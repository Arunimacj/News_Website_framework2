Microsoft Signs Nvidia Deal To Appease Antitrust Regulators

Microsoft gained support from Nvidia in its bid to purchase Activision Blizzard. Advertisements

Nvidia’s cloud gaming service, GeForce Now, will offer first-party Microsoft titles to over 20 million players for the coming decade.

Microsoft continually argues the Activision Blizzard merger would create competition in the video game industry. Activision Blizzard
also owns King, the creator of Candy Crush Saga. Microsoft hopes to bolster its position in the mobile gaming space to compete with
the likes of Tencent and Apple. And sales of Microsoft’s Xbox Series S/X consoles drastically lag behind Sony’s PlayStation 5 (PS5).

Microsoft President Brad Smith argued Sony holds a 70% global market share of the console industry. And it has maintained its
dominance as the video game console leader since Microsoft entered the market. Additionally, if the Activision Blizzard acquisition
is approved, Call of Duty will be available to even more players.
